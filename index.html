About Me

Hey, I am a doctoral researcher at the German Centre for Integrative Biodiversity Research (iDiv) Halle-Jena-Leipzig. My research focuses on investigating the mechanisms shaping complex species interactions. I investigate trophic interactions through multiple lenses: environmental drivers (e.g., temperature), predator-prey body-size ratios, and information flow among species, aiming to advance our understanding of species interacitons' formation, dynamics, and strength. I enjoy hiking (since living in Germany), detective novels (being a fan of Agatha and Anthony), and exploring tea (a passion rooted in my undergraduate studies).
